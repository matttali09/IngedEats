Top 5 Food Trends For 2016

Posted on February 2, 2016February 3, 2016 by hilarystreet27

1. Seaweed is the new Kale

Seaweed has been predicted to be the new super veggie for 2016 due to consumer’s demands for a more sustainable food source. Although kale is known for being extremely high in nutrients, seaweed has been able to surpass it in popularity due to it being packed full of fiber, antioxidants, iodine, and healthy fats. Seaweed is a plentiful food source around the world and is now being utilized to create new alternatives to the traditional lettuce or kale salads in the western world.

 

2. The Year Of The Pulse

Pulse grains are dried grains such as lentils, beans, peas, and chickpeas. The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations has established that 2016 is the year of pulse grains due to their high level of plant based protein and the ease of integrating them into daily meals as a substitute for meat-based dishes. Pulse grains are additionally a great way to maintain a healthy diet and also fight against obesity, diabetes, coronary conditions, and cancer.  Some great recipes include Chickpea salads, Pea Soup, and Lentil Stew.

3. Açaí Berry Bowls

The powerful super fruit has a new look for 2016 as a healthy snack alternative to ice cream or frozen yogurt. The açaí berry has gained popularity in the past few years due to its high level of antioxidants and healthy fats, and the easy of accessibility now as it is stocked in most grocery stores. A new twist on a frozen treat is the acai berry blended with other frozen fruit such as strawberries and bananas, then mixed with almond milk or coconut water and finished with your preference of toppings.

 

4. Ethnic Condiments

A new introduction into the condiment aisles this year are ethically inspired condiments from around the world; most recently due to the popularity of the hot sauce brand Sriracha. Exotic condiments such as the Korean Gochujang chili paste, Indonesian Sambal Oelek, and Chinese Black Bean Sauce are becoming more popular due to consumers searching for more hot alternatives compared to the classic ketchup and mustard. These new options can easily take tantalizing classic dishes to a whole new level of a spicy and flavorful experience.

 

5. Poke (Po-kay)

Known for being a traditional Hawaiian salad, this dish is taking the west coast by storm due to its flavorful ingredients and high level of protein. Poke is made with chunks of raw fish, the most popular options being Yuzu Salmon and Kale Snapper, and a vegan alternative being Shiitake Chili Tofu. These protein options are then combined with seasonings, sauce, and fruits and veggies on top of rice, salad, or noodles. Chefs are coming up with modern alternatives such as mixing the Poke with coconut, lime, jalapeno, and beets to shake up the classic Hawaiian dish. In Victoria, restaurants such as Shiki Sushi, Veneto, and Aura feature Poke on their menus and have adapted the dish to incorporate west coast flavours.

About Me and The Blog

Posted on January 30, 2016 by hilarystreet27

Marketing has never been a passion until now; the opportunity presented itself and I decided to take the chance to further develop my skill set for a future career.  I began my education in post-secondary with a diploma in Fashion Merchandising from Blanche MacDonald in Vancouver, which I finished in 2011. My original thought process was that I would take my diploma from Blanche, and be able to get a career immediately after graduation; however, this was not the case.   After working for two years in Vancouver, I decided to move back to Victoria with the goal of attending Camosun part time, while working full time at my new position as Keyholder at Club Monaco.  After several months, I decided I needed a change; taking on a new position at BCBG MaxAzria, and pursuing my studies full time.

Currently, I am going into my 4th year of studies in the Business Administration- Marketing Degree program, and my expected graduation date is December 2016. Once finished my degree, my aspirations are to obtain a position related to operations management and company organization.

Whisk and Spatula’s goal is to inspire individuals on the latest cooking and baking trends that have developed through marketing. I selected this topic because I have a love for baking and I am looking to try out new recipes this year as a part of my New Year’s resolution. I have a keen interest in improving my cooking skills, especially with a priority on making healthy alternatives to traditional meals. The world of cooking is constantly updating in techniques and alternatives, especially with the development of social media resources such as Facebook and YouTube. Inspiring chefs are able to post their tips and techniques through inspiring videos and photos that help guide their audiences towards making small changes towards healthier lifestyles. By living on the west coast, we have many opportunities to incorporate fresh locally grown produce into our cooking everyday, and even the chance to grow it ourselves.
